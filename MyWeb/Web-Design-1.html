Equipment Compliance & Teacher Accountability

Because the Borrowing Teacher is officially responsible for all gear, student non-compliance will result in the following graduated consequences for that teacher’s account:


Offense: Formal Warning
The Issue: Minor lateness or equipment left disorganized.
The Consequence: A formal notification is sent to the borrowing teacher. The student must perform a "Gear Check" (organizing the equipment) before the next session.


2nd Offense: 48-Hour Suspension
The Issue: Equipment returned damaged due to misuse or significantly late.
The Consequence: The borrowing teacher’s privilege is suspended for 2 school days. No students from that teacher's class may borrow gear during this "cool-down" period.


3rd Offense: Week-Long Ban & Report
The Issue: Repeated negligence or failure to return a high-value item (e.g., a jersey or specialized racket).
The Consequence: A 7-day ban on all borrowing for that teacher. A report is filed with the Department Head noting that the teacher is currently "non-compliant" with equipment safety standards.


4th Offense: Permanent Revocation & Replacement Cost
The Issue: Loss of equipment or total disregard for borrowing rules.
The Consequence: The teacher’s borrowing privileges are revoked indefinitely. To restore compliance, the teacher (or their department) must provide a replacement for the lost item or meet with Administration to discuss a new supervision plan.


Message to Students:
"Your teacher’s professional record and your class's fun are on the line. Every ball, bat, and bib must be accounted for. If you don't comply, your teacher is the one who pays the price. Don't be the reason your class loses their gear."
